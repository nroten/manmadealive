Section 03 · Voice & Terminology

Pastoral. Masculine.
Rooted in formation.

We speak with care, not pressure. We name the hard things, but never harden against the man we're speaking to. Every line of copy should be defensible by all four of these principles at once.

◆ Pastoral, not salesy

We speak with care, not pressure. Language is invitational, grounded in Scripture. Calls are extended, not pushed.

◆ Masculine, not aggressive

Rugged and honest. We don't soften the call, but we extend it with grace. Strength under submission, not strength performed.

◆ Formation, not information

MMA is about becoming, not just learning. Every touchpoint reflects a journey. No motivational sugar. Slow, steady work.

◆ Rooted, not trendy

Our language draws from Scripture and historic theology, not cultural fads. Irenaeus and Paul carry the ground; not influencers.

◆ Key Terminology · canonical
Formation
The ongoing process of becoming the man God made you to be. The opposite of information.
Drift
The slow, unnoticed move away from intentional biblical living. One degree off, sustained over distance.
Fog
The lived experience of drift. The low, specific tension a man can't name but knows is there.
The Jolt
The moment of recognition — hearing your father's words come out of your mouth, the silence after a hard reaction.
Training
Replacing "trying harder" with structured, sustainable growth. Capacity grows. Now the weight that used to crush him moves.
Brotherhood
Men walking together in Christ — not accountability for its own sake. No man is formed alone.
The Three Stories
Soften / Harden / Erase. The cultural visions of manhood that steep into a man without his permission.
River with Banks
Strength under submission. Power not diminished by direction — concentrated by it. The Christ-pattern.
The Pattern
Christ. Not a role model to imitate, but the standard that transforms a man as he beholds it.
Personal Formation Charter
A one-page living document holding where a man is + the man he's choosing to become. Free, no strings.
Stay Steady
The closing benediction on every video and page. Pastoral, masculine, restrained. Never substitute.
The Hollow Feeling
What every cultural story produces. The room is full and something in him is still empty.

Section 04 · Component Vocabulary

The pieces
a page is built from.

Every page assembled from this kit looks like the same brand. Reach for these patterns first; invent only when none of them fit.

01

Italic-accent headline

Every H1 contains exactly one italic Newsreader word or phrase, gold-coloured. The italic carries the emotional weight; the rest is structural.
The man you were
made to become.
02

Eyebrow tag & brand chip row

A pulsing pill above every hero (◆ MMA · Module · Video) plus 3–4 hero chips that frame the audience. Mostly grey, with one green chip (the hopeful anchor) and one gold chip (the warning anchor).
For the man still showing up Still working hard Still in the pew Still feels off
03

Pull quote

Newsreader italic, oversized, centred, with a single gold-coloured key clause. Use ~once per ~3 sections as a soundbite break.
"None of them begins with God."
04

Promise / Produces card pair

Used to dismantle a false story or pattern. Promise on the left (gold-light, what it claims). Produces on the right (red, what it actually delivers).
◆ Promises
Peace. Through avoidance, accommodation, keeping the volume low.
▲ Produces
A man present in the room and missing from the moment.
05

RAG / Build-Wait-Skip cards

Three pulsing-dot cards for honest self-assessment. Green = ready / step in. Amber = wait / triage first. Red = skip / not for you. Use to qualify the audience without pressuring.
If you've been quietly carrying this — start here.
In acute crisis right now — get triage first.
Looking for techniques only — this isn't the program.
06

Custom SVG diagram

For each video's centerpiece metaphor, render a painterly editorial SVG as the visual proof: 1-in-60 ship route (V1), lie-detector baseline + wood-grain (V2), river-with-banks (V3). Always two-panel comparisons (gold/green = right / red = wrong). Animated stroke-dash on the "right" path.

Section 05 · Metaphor Library & Rituals

The images
worth carrying.

Brand language draws from a fixed library of metaphors — each carries a specific theological weight. Pull from this list first; never invent a metaphor for a concept already named here.

V1
Driving through fog

The opening image. The low, specific tension a man can't name. Hands on wheel, family in the car.

V1
One degree off · 1-in-60 rule

NY → London → France. Drift is a heading problem, not a horsepower problem.

V1
Archer with no target

Effort released into infinite mist. Most men aren't lacking effort — they lack a target worth releasing toward.

V1
The empty tank

Running on fumes. He looks like he doesn't care; the tank hit zero years ago. Build capacity before you lead anyone.

V1
The old wound

A father's silence. A failure carried alone. Self-doubt as the water he swims in. Formation begins where a man actually is.

V2
Tea bag steeping

Culture shapes a man without his permission. The water doesn't decide what colour it becomes.

V2
The jolt

The moment of recognition. Your father's words in your mouth. The car in the driveway. That moment has a name.

V2
Lie detector baseline

Without a calibrated standard, any confident story passes for true. Most men received cultural visions before they could evaluate them.

V2
Three stories: soften · harden · erase

Passive / dominant / apologetic. Each one promises something. Each one quietly insufficient.

V2
Wood grain · against the grain

The wood doesn't stop you — it splinters. A man living against his design frays slowly, not all at once.

V3
River with banks

Power concentrated, not diminished. The Christ-pattern. Strength under submission, authority in service.

V3
The forge

Heat. Time. The willingness to stay in it long enough for something real to happen. Formation, not imitation.

V3
The temple whip

Aftermath of a moment when something needed to be set right. Strength used purposefully, never reactively.

V3
Brotherhood walking

Men on the same path, no one alone, no one out front. Faithful and unhurried.

Visual Rituals · always-on
◆ Italic accent Every hero contains exactly one italic Newsreader phrase. The italic is where the heart of the line lives.
◆ The closer "Until then — stay steady." Every video, every page, every email signoff. Pastoral. Masculine. Never substituted.
◆ Eyebrow tag Above every hero: ◆ Man Made Alive · <Series> · <Video N of N>. Pulsing gold pill, Oswald 11/0.22em.
◆ Hero chip row 3–4 audience chips below the hero standfirst. One green chip as the hopeful anchor; one gold chip as the tension anchor.
