Section 01

My Current
Reality.

Before a man can aim, he has to see clearly where he actually is. These questions are not meant to discourage — they are meant to lift the fog.

01

Where in my life am I moving forward without a clear sense of where I'm actually headed?

Think about the areas that feel heaviest right now — work, marriage, fatherhood, faith, friendships.

02

Where does my strength feel misaligned — spent on things that don't reflect what actually matters to me?

03

What has most shaped my understanding of what it means to be a man — and is that something I would actually choose?

Consider your father, your peers, your culture, your failures. Which story have you been living by?

Return to these when you feel stuck, scattered, or numb. They will tell you something true — which is always the beginning of finding your way forward.

My Compass
Bearing.

A compass doesn't show you every step — it tells you which direction is north. This is your bearing. It will sharpen as you grow. For now, begin with what is honest.

01

Write in full sentences. Not what sounds right — what is actually true for you right now.

02

The pattern I am choosing to aim my life toward is the pattern of Christ. What does that specifically mean in my life right now?

Not a theological answer — a personal one. What would a man being formed into his likeness look like in your marriage, your work, your home?
